How it works

Five steps, never alone at any of them

The same arc every successful acquisition follows — with your broker carrying the heavy parts.

  1. Meet your broker

    A matching call about budget, hours and goals pairs you with one named broker — yours for the whole journey.

  2. Shortlist together

    Your broker screens the market and brings you a handful of real fits, each with an honest first-pass read.

  3. Offer with backup

    Pricing, terms and the LOI drafted with you — and a steady voice when the negotiation gets noisy.

  4. Diligence, translated

    Verification runs in the background; your broker turns findings into plain-English decisions.

  5. Close, then land

    Escrowed funds, guided transfer, and a 30-day handover window so week one feels like week ten.

Broker support

What "matched broker" actually means

Not a chat widget. Not a rotating queue. One person who knows your budget, your blind spots, and the names of the businesses keeping you up at night.

  • Buyer-side only — your broker negotiates against the seller, never for them.
  • Plain-English everything — multiples, add-backs and escrow explained like a friend would.
  • Honest "pass" advice — the cheapest deal is the bad one you never buy.
  • Financing & deal support — seller notes, earn-outs and funding paths structured for you.

A week with your broker, roughly

Mon

Shortlist review: two new fits, one removed — with reasons you can sanity-check.

Wed

Seller-call debrief: what they said, what the numbers say, where those differ.

Fri

Tracker update: every stage current, one next step per deal, nothing floating.

Any

The "is this normal?" text message. Yes, you can send it. Everyone does.
