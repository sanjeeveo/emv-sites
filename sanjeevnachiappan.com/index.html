Profile

About Sanjeev Nachiappan

Sanjeev Nachiappan is an operator-investor based in Beverly Hills, California, and the founder and chief executive of Exchange Media Ventures (EMV) — the holding company behind a twelve-brand portfolio of digital businesses. He works at the point where ownership meets operations: buying, building, and scaling internet companies that most investors consider too small to notice and most owners find too complex to grow alone.

His thesis is operator-led micro-acquisition. Rather than financial engineering, Sanjeev Nachiappan acquires small, profitable digital businesses — Shopify stores, SaaS products, and online marketplaces — and grows them with hands-on operating playbooks: sharper merchandising, cleaner unit economics, disciplined channel expansion, and teams that stay accountable long after a deal closes.

Today he runs the EMV portfolio of twelve brands spanning the full ownership cycle of a digital business — valuation through Dexits; scaling through ScaleUni, ScaleVerticals, Scalifty, and ItScaled; exits through ExitWebsites, OnlinExit, and Wexits; and acquisition through AquireOnline, AquireWebsites, and OnlineAquisition — all coordinated from EMV's Beverly Hills office.

Philosophy

How Sanjeev Nachiappan works

Three principles run through every EMV brand — from a $7-a-month valuation app to a concierge exit engagement.

I.

Operator-bought, not financialized

EMV buys businesses to run them, not to mark them up. Every acquisition decision starts with an operating plan — what we will actually change in the first ninety days — rather than a spreadsheet of synergies that never ship.

II.

Education-first scaling

The playbooks are taught openly through ScaleUni before they are sold as services. An owner who understands exactly how their business grows is a better partner, a better seller, and a better buyer.

III.

Aligned incentives

Profit-share models like Scalifty mean EMV earns when the owner earns. Across valuation, scaling, and exits, the portfolio is structured so the advice and the outcome point the same direction.
