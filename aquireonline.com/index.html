The buying process

From criteria to keys

A marketplace on the surface, a deal machine underneath. Here's the route every Aqure buyer travels.

  1. Set your criteria

    Budget, asset class, niche, risk tolerance, hours per week. The matcher starts learning immediately.

  2. Review surfaced fits

    Listings arrive ranked by match score. Save what intrigues you, pass on what doesn't — both teach the engine.

  3. Verify with confidence

    Open the data room behind the Verified Revenue badge and trace every number to its source.

  4. Offer with a broker

    Your broker prices the offer, drafts the LOI, and manages the back-and-forth so emotion never sets the terms.

  5. Close and transfer

    Escrowed funds, asset-by-asset migration checklist, and a handover window with the seller on call.

Off-market desk

We broker any business — even ones not officially for sale.

Found a store, tool or newsletter you'd love to own that isn't listed anywhere? Give us the URL — or just the profile. Our off-market desk approaches owners discreetly on your behalf: no public listing, no bidding war, no awkward first email from you.

Plenty of great digital businesses never reach a marketplace. We knock anyway.

    NDA-first outreach from our desk, never your name in the opening note. Owners can say no — many say "tell me more."

    If they'll talk, the conversation enters the same brokered process as any listed deal — verification included.

Broker support

Brokered, not browsed-and-hoped

Most marketplaces hand you a chat thread and wish you luck. On Aqure, a named broker is attached to your account from your first shortlist — buyer-side, always.

Meet the people behind it
  • Valuation sanity-check — is the asking multiple fair for this asset class right now?
  • Offer & LOI drafting — terms, contingencies and timelines written to protect you.
  • Diligence coordination — access requests, document chase, and red-flag triage handled for you.
  • Escrow & transfer — funds release only as assets actually move to your accounts.
  • Post-close handover — a structured window with the seller so nothing breaks in week one.
