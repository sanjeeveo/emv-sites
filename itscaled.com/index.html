How the handover works

Three phases. One of them is yours. Briefly.

  1. Hand it over

    One kickoff session. Our checklist collects access, assets, and context in a single sitting, and the first sprint gets mapped before the call ends. That is the last setup task you will do.

  2. We build

    Sprint zero ships the foundations fast: tracking that tells the truth, the first offer or funnel rebuild, a creative pipeline, and the email spine. Days in, not months in.

  3. It ships, weekly

    From then on the studio runs on a weekly release rhythm. Every Friday your changelog lands: what went live, what is queued, and the one approval we need — answerable from your phone.

The differentiator

Execution ownership, documented every Friday

Most growth partners send reports about meetings. We send receipts of shipped work. The changelog is a standing, written record that the studio moved your business forward this week — or owes you the reason why.

  • Shipped beats planned: the log lists what went live, never what got discussed
  • Numbers ride along: each entry links to the metric it is meant to move
  • One ask, maximum: at most a single approval request per week, sized for a phone screen
  • No silent weeks: the log arrives every Friday, glamorous sprint or not
